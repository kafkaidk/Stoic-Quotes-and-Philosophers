About Marcus Aurelius

Marcus Aurelius, born in 121 AD, was a Roman Emperor and Stoic philosopher who ruled the Roman Empire from 161 to 180 AD. He is best known for his work "Meditations," a series of personal writings reflecting his Stoic philosophy. In "Meditations," Marcus Aurelius provides insights into his thoughts on virtue, morality, and the nature of existence. Despite the challenges of leading an empire, he emphasized the importance of self-discipline, rationality, and maintaining inner tranquility. Marcus Aurelius' writings continue to inspire and guide individuals seeking wisdom in their daily lives.
